Welcome to My Teaching Portfolio!

Dr. Mustafa Al Lail is an Associate Professor in the School of Engineering at Texas A&M International University (TAMIU). Throughout his career, he has had the opportunity to learn from and contribute to various institutions. This experience includes serving as visiting faculty at esteemed institutions like Oak Ridge National Laboratory and Colorado State University and holding a faculty position at Seattle University before joining TAMIU.

Dr. Al Lail's academic journey has been enriching. He holds Master's and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from Colorado State University, where he benefited from the guidance of distinguished professors: Dr. Indrakshi Ray, a cybersecurity expert, and Dr. Robert B. France, a leader in software engineering research. His foundation began with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from the well-regarded King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals in Dhahran, Saudi Arabia.

Teaching Accomplishment Highlights

I am pleased that you are taking the time to review my teaching portfolio. I have carefully compiled this portfolio to showcase my teaching philosophy. I invite you to explore the various sections of my portfolio at your own pace. I have made an effort to present the information clearly and concisely. However, please feel free to reach out if you have any questions or need further clarification on any aspect of my portfolio. Feel free to explore the tabs at the top of the page to easily navigate through my portfolio. Below, I have shared some highlights of my teaching accomplishments that I hope you'll enjoy reading!

My commitment to teaching excellence is reflected in a strong record of accomplishments.

  • Student Success and Growth: I am deeply gratified to have mentored over 50 undergraduate research assistants, guiding their research endeavors and fostering their intellectual growth. I am proud of their accomplishments, which include:
    • Securing prestigious positions: Five of my mentees have secured prestigious internships and permanent roles at DOE National Laboratories, while four have gained valuable research experience through internships in France.
    • Pursuing advanced degrees: Eight of my mentees have successfully pursued graduate studies, enrolling in Master's and Ph.D. programs at leading institutions.
    • Publishing scientific research: Since 2018, I have fostered a strong research environment at TAMIU, resulting in 14 undergraduate students co-authoring 20 peer-reviewed publications in conferences and journals. Notably, several students have contributed to multiple publications, demonstrating significant growth as researchers.
    • Achieving success in industry: The remaining mentees have embarked on successful careers at top-tier companies within the technology sector.
    These achievements are a testament to my students' hard work and dedication. I am honored to have played a role in their academic and professional development.
    Furthermore, my courses consistently receive high student evaluations, with an average rating of 4.7 out of 5. This positive feedback from students underscores their high satisfaction with the learning experience and indicates a positive and engaging classroom environment.
  • Curriculum Development: I have actively contributed to developing several innovative academic programs at TAMIU, including the B.S. in Computer Engineering, the B.S. in Computer Science, the certificate in Artificial Intelligence, and the certificate in Cybersecurity in Criminal Justice. These programs are designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in the rapidly evolving fields.
    Furthermore, I have developed 12 new courses within our programs, incorporating modern teaching methodologies and utilizing the latest technologies to create engaging and effective learning experiences. Throughout the 2022-2023 academic year, I served as the primary instructor for the B.S. in Computer Engineering program’s flagship course, CSCE 4390: Computer Engineering Senior Design Project. I collaborated with the program’s first class of seniors on their graduation projects. My efforts have positioned the program favorably regarding its Senior Design profile and have helped it gain ABET accreditation.
  • Professional Development: I am committed to continuous professional development and strive to enhance my teaching practices through ongoing learning. I have actively participated in a variety of professional development activities, including:
    • ACUE Effective College Instruction: As a member of the inaugural cohort, I successfully completed this year-long program, gaining valuable insights into effective teaching methodologies, student learning, and classroom management.
    • Quality Matters Certification: I have earned Quality Matters certification, demonstrating my commitment to designing and delivering high-quality online courses.
    • Top Hat Certification: I have completed Top Hat certification, enhancing my proficiency in utilizing this platform to create engaging and interactive learning experiences for my students.
    • Artificial Intelligence in Education: I have participated in training programs focused on integrating Artificial Intelligence in education, exploring innovative ways to leverage AI to enhance student learning and personalize the learning experience.
    • Numerous Workshops and Seminars: I have consistently engaged in professional development opportunities, attending workshops and seminars on active learning strategies, assessment techniques, inclusive teaching practices, and student success.
    These professional development experiences have significantly enriched my teaching practice, enabling me to create a more engaging, effective, and inclusive learning environment for all students.
  • Technological Innovation: I am a strong advocate for the effective integration of technology in education. I have effectively utilized platforms like Top Hat, Echo360, and zyBooks to enhance student engagement, facilitate interactive learning, and personalize the learning experience for each student.
  • Leadership and Mentorship: I believe in fostering a collaborative and supportive environment within the academic community. I have actively mentored several colleagues, sharing my expertise in teaching methodologies, technology integration, and curriculum development. These mentorship experiences have been rewarding, allowing me to contribute to the professional growth of my colleagues and enhance the overall quality of teaching and learning within the department.

These accomplishments demonstrate my dedication to providing a high-quality and engaging learning experience for my students, and my commitment to continuous improvement in my teaching practices.

Synergetic Activities and Relationship to the Mission of TAMIU

As a Minority-Serving Institution, Texas A&M International University (TAMIU) serves a diverse student body, with 89.3% identifying as Hispanic, 76% coming from low-income backgrounds, and over 51% being first-generation college students. Through instruction, research, and public service, TAMIU's mission to improve the quality of life for citizens in the border region, the State of Texas, and national and international communities resonates deeply with my professional values. I joined TAMIU because of the strong alignment between my mission as a professor and the university's mission and strategic plan, which emphasizes visionary academic programs and faculty, empowered student success, and advancing research excellence. My research, teaching, and service activities all focus on empowering students to become agents of positive change. 

My contributions to TAMIU's mission are multifaceted. A significant achievement has been my involvement in establishing the new Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Engineering, which has seen a remarkable growth of 165% since its launch in the 2020-2021 academic year. This program addresses the need for skilled engineers in our region and beyond. Furthermore, I have mentored over twenty-five undergraduate research projects, providing invaluable research experience to diverse students, including many Hispanic scholars, and preparing them for future success in graduate school or the workforce. My commitment to research excellence is also evident in my efforts to expand collaborative and interdisciplinary research opportunities, collaborating with scholars from TAMIU, R1 research universities, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and international partners. These collaborations advance knowledge in my field and provide enriching opportunities for our students.

These synergistic activities demonstrate my dedication to TAMIU's mission. I am committed to continuing my work at TAMIU, which has become a second home, and further contributing to the success of our students and the communities we serve. My portfolio reflects my commitment to TAMIU and its mission, and I welcome any questions or requests for further information. I am grateful for the support of the TAMIU family and look forward to serving our students and the university for many years to come.
